# The Ultimate Vendor Selection Guide
## How to Find, Vet, and Book Your Dream Team

**By Emily Experience**

Choosing the right vendors can make or break your event. This comprehensive guide will teach you everything you need to know about finding, interviewing, and booking the perfect vendors for your special day.

---

## 🔍 The Vetting Process

### Step 1: Initial Research
**Where to Look:**
- Emily Experience vendor marketplace (verified and reviewed)
- Wedding/event websites (The Knot, WeddingWire)
- Instagram and Pinterest
- Friends and family recommendations
- Venue preferred vendor lists

**Red Flags to Watch For:**
- No website or online presence
- No reviews or testimonials
- Prices significantly below market rate
- Reluctance to provide references
- No contract or vague terms
- Poor communication during inquiry

## ❓ Essential Interview Questions

### For All Vendors

**Experience & Availability:**
1. "How many events have you done in the past year?"
2. "Are you available on our date?"
3. "Will you be the person working our event, or will it be an associate?"
4. "What happens if you're sick or have an emergency on our date?"

**Logistics:**
5. "What's included in your package?"
6. "Are there any additional fees we should know about?"
7. "How much is the deposit, and when is the final payment due?"
8. "What's your cancellation policy?"

**Communication:**
9. "How quickly do you typically respond to emails?"
10. "Will we have a final meeting before the event?"
11. "What's the best way to reach you?"

---

### Venue-Specific Questions

**Logistics:**
- "What's the capacity for seated vs. cocktail-style events?"
- "Do you have a rain plan for outdoor spaces?"
- "What time can vendors arrive for setup?"
- "Is there a noise ordinance or curfew?"
- "Are there any decoration restrictions?"

**Services:**
- "Do you provide tables, chairs, and linens?"
- "Do you have in-house catering, or can we bring our own?"
- "Is there a bridal suite or getting-ready space?"
- "How many restrooms are available?"
- "Is there parking for guests?"

**Costs:**
- "What's included in the rental fee?"
- "Are there hidden fees (service charges, gratuity, cleanup)?"
- "Do you require event insurance?"

---

### Photographer-Specific Questions

**Style & Portfolio:**
- "Can we see full galleries from recent events?"
- "How would you describe your photography style?"
- "Do you shoot film, digital, or both?"
- "How do you handle low-light situations?"

**Deliverables:**
- "How many edited photos will we receive?"
- "How long does editing take?"
- "Do we get printing rights?"
- "Do you offer albums or prints?"
- "Will you publish our photos online?"

**Coverage:**
- "How many hours are included?"
- "Do you bring a second shooter?"
- "Do you cover getting-ready photos?"
- "What happens if you exceed the contracted hours?"

---

### Caterer-Specific Questions

**Food & Menu:**
- "Can we customize the menu?"
- "Do you accommodate dietary restrictions (vegan, gluten-free, allergies)?"
- "Can we do a tasting before booking?"
- "Where do you source your ingredients?"

**Service:**
- "What's the staff-to-guest ratio?"
- "How do you handle late guests?"
- "Do you provide cake cutting service?"
- "Do you handle cleanup and breakdown?"

**Logistics:**
- "Do you need kitchen access?"
- "How much setup time do you need?"
- "Do you provide linens, plates, and silverware?"
- "Can we provide our own alcohol?"

## 📝 Contract Checklist

### Every Contract Should Include:

**Basic Information:**
- [ ] Date, time, and location of event
- [ ] Vendor's full legal business name and contact info
- [ ] Your full names and contact info

**Services & Deliverables:**
- [ ] Detailed description of services
- [ ] Specific hours of coverage/service
- [ ] Number of staff/crew
- [ ] What's included vs. add-ons

**Payment Terms:**
- [ ] Total cost and itemized breakdown
- [ ] Deposit amount and due date
- [ ] Final payment due date
- [ ] Accepted payment methods
- [ ] Late payment penalties

**Cancellation & Changes:**
- [ ] Cancellation policy (both sides)
- [ ] Refund schedule
- [ ] Rescheduling policy
- [ ] Force majeure clause

**Legal Protection:**
- [ ] Liability insurance requirements
- [ ] Indemnification clauses
- [ ] Damage deposit (if applicable)
- [ ] Arbitration/mediation clauses

## ⚠️ Red Flags That Mean Walk Away

### Major Warning Signs:
1. **Pressure tactics** – "Book today or lose the date"
2. **Cash only** – Requiring cash with no receipt
3. **No physical address** – Only a PO box or vague location
4. **Reluctance to meet** – Won't meet in person or video call
5. **Unprofessional communication** – Poor grammar, slow responses, rude tone
6. **Too good to be true pricing** – Significantly below market rate
7. **No contract** – Verbal agreements only
8. **Bad reviews with no response** – Ignores negative feedback
9. **Changing terms** – Keeps altering what's included
10. **No backup plan** – No contingency for emergencies

## ✅ Final Verification Steps

### Before You Sign:
- [ ] Read the entire contract (don't just skim)
- [ ] Check references (call at least 3 past clients)
- [ ] Visit their business location (if applicable)
- [ ] Verify insurance and licenses
- [ ] Compare with 2-3 other vendors
- [ ] Trust your gut feeling
- [ ] Get everything in writing
- [ ] Keep copies of all communication

## 🎯 Negotiation Tips

### What's Negotiable:
- Package customization
- Off-peak pricing (Friday/Sunday vs. Saturday)
- Off-season discounts
- Payment terms
- Additional services/inclusions

### What's NOT Negotiable:
- Base prices (disrespectful to ask)
- Safety requirements
- Insurance requirements
- Legal contract terms

### How to Ask:
- "Is there any flexibility in the pricing?"
- "What if we booked for [off-peak date]?"
- "Could we customize this package to fit our needs?"
- "Are there any current promotions?"

---

## 📞 Emergency Backup Plan

### Always Have:
1. **Backup vendor list** – 2-3 alternatives for each category
2. **Emergency contacts** – Available on event day
3. **Contracts saved** – Physical and digital copies
4. **Vendor insurance info** – Confirm coverage amounts
5. **Day-of coordinator** – Someone to handle issues

### If Your Vendor Cancels:
1. Check contract for cancellation clauses
2. Contact backup vendors immediately
3. Notify venue and other vendors
4. Document all communication
5. Consider legal action if necessary

---

## 🌟 Final Tips for Success

1. **Start early** – Best vendors book 9-12 months ahead
2. **Trust reviews** – But read them critically
3. **Meet in person** – Chemistry matters
4. **Get it in writing** – Verbal promises mean nothing
5. **Follow your budget** – Don't be upsold
6. **Communicate clearly** – Over-communicate, don't assume
7. **Be respectful** – Professional courtesy goes both ways
8. **Plan for problems** – Things can go wrong, be prepared
9. **Enjoy the process** – Vendor selection can be fun!
10. **Trust your instincts** – If something feels off, walk away
